Our Collections

The Université de Montréal Libraries develop and maintain rich and diverse collections to support teaching, research, and the creation of knowledge within the university community.

Books, journals, theses, databases, images, datasets, and other specialized resources are made available in both print and digital formats, with a focus on accessibility, sustainability, and scholarly quality.

A rigorous and collaborative selection process

Collection development decisions are based on continuous assessment of the community’s needs. In 2022, an institutional consultation identified core journal titles essential to teaching and research, taking into account usage data, citation analysis, and disciplinary recommendations.

This collaborative approach guides acquisition decisions while ensuring balance across disciplines and responsible management of financial resources.

Use of electronic resources

Most electronic resources provided by the Libraries are available through subscription and governed by license agreements. Access is restricted to the UdeM community, including students, faculty, researchers, and staff.

Licenses allow users to consult and download content for personal teaching and research purposes. Some licenses also permit the integration of articles or chapters into StudiUM or course packs intended for the UdeM community.

Commercial use, large-scale downloading, or sharing resources with unauthorized users is prohibited and may result in suspension of access.

Collection policies and library services

The Libraries prioritize digital formats in their acquisition processes in order to support remote access, simultaneous use, and the long-term preservation of documentary resources.
